Martial Arts Journey

I joined Hobin Fighter Silat Club two semesters ago to learn traditional Malay martial arts and improve my physical fitness.

Although I'm relatively new to Silat, I've been actively participating in training sessions and learning the basic techniques and forms (gerakan).

One of my notable experiences was trying out for the SUPER UiTM Selection under Seremban. Although I wasn't selected, the experience taught me valuable lessons about perseverance and sportsmanship.

"Silat is not just about physical strength, but also about mental discipline and character building."

Silat Training Details

  • Duration: 2 Semesters
  • Focus: Basic techniques & self-defense
  • Training: Group sessions twice a week
  • Competition: SUPER UiTM tryouts (Seremban)
  • Benefits: Fitness & discipline
  • Goal: Master basic Silat forms
